Piecewice Constant Curvature Soft Robotic Arm Geometrics and Dynamics

Created2025-01-17|Updated2025-01-18|physics
|Post Views:

Geometrics

Space Definition

Each segment has id kkk (k = 1, 2, 3 …), in our current model it is k=2. Each segment kkk is modelled by three actuation variables. jjj means the jjj-th actuation length (jjj = 1, 2, 3). The actuation length is not the same as the stick length. The variables in actuation and configuration space can be expressed as LkL_kLk​ and QkQ_kQk​ respectively.

Actuation Space

actuation space is consist of 3 actuators’ length lk1l_{k1}lk1​, lk2l_{k2}lk2​, and lk3l_{k3}lk3​.

Configuration Space

configuration space for our fix-base robotic arm is Qk=(κk,ϕk,βk)Q_k = (\kappa_k, \phi_k, \beta_k)Qk​=(κk​,ϕk​,βk​) which is the same as the spherical coordinate.
And κ\kappaκ denotes the curvature and ϕ\phiϕ denotes rotation angle about z-axis.
Or κ\kappaκ can be expressed as the arc length LCk=ρk⋅βkL_{Ck} = \rho_k\cdot \beta_kLCk​=ρk​⋅βk​, and that LCkL_{Ck}LCk​ can be expressed as the arm center’s length which is the average of the 3 origamis’ length.
The equations and images are shown below:

  1. LCk=(LC1+LC2+LC3)/3L_{Ck} = (L_{C1} + L_{C2} + L_{C3})/3LCk​=(LC1​+LC2​+LC3​)/3
  2. Lkj=LCk−ΔkjβkL_{kj} = L_{Ck} -\Delta_{kj}\beta_kLkj​=LCk​−Δkj​βk​ where Δkj=rcos⁡(φkj−ϕk)\Delta_{kj} = r\cos (\varphi_{kj} - \phi_k)Δkj​=rcos(φkj​−ϕk​) and the angles definitions are shown in the figure below.

    and φkj=(j−1)⋅2π/3\varphi_{kj} = (j - 1) \cdot 2\pi /3φkj​=(j−1)⋅2π/3

Rotations and Positions

the rotation can be expressed as 3 unit rotations about Z axis -> Y axis -> Z axis.
Thus the rotation can be expressed as:

Rkk−1=Rot(Zk−1,ϕk)Rot(Yk−1,βk)Rot(Zk,−ϕk)R_{k}^{k-1}= Rot(Z_{k-1}, \phi_k) Rot(Y_{k-1}, \beta_k) Rot(Z_k, -\phi_k) Rkk−1​=Rot(Zk−1​,ϕk​)Rot(Yk−1​,βk​)Rot(Zk​,−ϕk​)

where kkk is the section id, and the resulting matrix is:

[cos⁡βk(cos⁡ϕk)2+(sin⁡ϕk)2(−1+cos⁡βk)cos⁡ϕksin⁡ϕkcos⁡ϕksin⁡βk(−1+cos⁡βk)cos⁡ϕksin⁡ϕk(cos⁡ϕk)2+cos⁡βk(sin⁡ϕk)2sin⁡ϕksin⁡βk−cos⁡ϕksin⁡βk−sin⁡βksin⁡ϕkcos⁡βk]\begin{bmatrix} \cos\beta_k (\cos\phi_k)^2 + (\sin\phi_k)^2 & (-1 + \cos\beta_k)\cos\phi_k\sin\phi_k & \cos\phi_k\sin\beta_k \\ (-1 + \cos\beta_k)\cos\phi_k\sin\phi_k & (\cos\phi_k)^2 + \cos\beta_k (\sin\phi_k)^2 & \sin\phi_k\sin\beta_k \\ -\cos\phi_k\sin\beta_k & -\sin\beta_k\sin\phi_k & \cos\beta_k \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎢⎡​cosβk​(cosϕk​)2+(sinϕk​)2(−1+cosβk​)cosϕk​sinϕk​−cosϕk​sinβk​​(−1+cosβk​)cosϕk​sinϕk​(cosϕk​)2+cosβk​(sinϕk​)2−sinβk​sinϕk​​cosϕk​sinβk​sinϕk​sinβk​cosβk​​⎦⎥⎤​

And then for the position vector it is

Pkk−1=1ρk[(1−cos⁡βk)cos⁡ϕk, (1−cos⁡βk)sin⁡ϕk, sin⁡βk]T,\mathbf{P}_k^{k-1} = \frac{1}{\rho_k} \begin{bmatrix} (1 - \cos\beta_k)\cos\phi_k ,\ (1 - \cos\beta_k)\sin\phi_k ,\ \sin\beta_k \end{bmatrix}^T, Pkk−1​=ρk​1​[(1−cosβk​)cosϕk​, (1−cosβk​)sinϕk​, sinβk​​]T,

And putting them together in 1 transformation matrix is like:

Tkk−1= [Rkk−1Pkk−101]4×4\mathbf{T}_k^{k-1} =  \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{R}_k^{k-1} & \mathbf{P}_k^{k-1} \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}_{4 \times 4} Tkk−1​= [Rkk−1​0​Pkk−1​1​]4×4​

the above matrix shows the transformation from the k-th arm section to the (k+1)-th arm section, namely we keep right multiply the transformation matrix from the initial base coordinate and get:

Tn0=T10T21⋯Tnn−1=[Rn0Pn001] \mathbf{T}_n^0 = \mathbf{T}_1^0 \mathbf{T}_2^1 \cdots \mathbf{T}_n^{n-1} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{R}_n^0 & \mathbf{P}_n^0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}Tn0​=T10​T21​⋯Tnn−1​=[Rn0​0​Pn0​1​]
